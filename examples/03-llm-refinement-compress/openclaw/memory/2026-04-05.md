Incident response support this week. A user's production API started
returning 502s after a deployment. Walked through the debugging process:

1. Checked ALB target group health — all targets unhealthy
2. Checked ECS service events — tasks failing health checks
3. Found the issue: new container image had a misconfigured port
   (listening on 3000, health check hitting 8080)
4. Rolled back to the previous task definition, service recovered

Recommended they add a pre-deployment health check to their CI/CD
pipeline to catch port mismatches before they hit production.

Also revisited the multi-cloud Kubernetes setup from March. The team
has GKE Autopilot running as primary and EKS as secondary. They're
seeing about 15% higher costs on the EKS side due to node management
overhead. Suggested looking at Karpenter to close the gap.
