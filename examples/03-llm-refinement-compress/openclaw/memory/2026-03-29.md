Cost optimization week. Three separate users asked about reducing their
AWS bills. Common patterns I keep seeing:

1. Running dev/staging environments 24/7 when they're only used during
   business hours. Recommended scheduled scaling or environment shutdown
   scripts. One user was spending $800/mo on a staging cluster that was
   idle 16 hours a day.

2. Not using Savings Plans or Reserved Instances for predictable
   workloads. Walked two users through the Savings Plans calculator.

3. Over-provisioned RDS instances. One team was running a db.r5.2xlarge
   for a database that peaks at 15% CPU. Recommended downsizing to
   db.r5.large and monitoring for two weeks.

Also recommended EKS with Karpenter to a team that was frustrated with
cluster autoscaler's slow scaling. Karpenter provisions nodes in under
60 seconds vs 3-5 minutes.
